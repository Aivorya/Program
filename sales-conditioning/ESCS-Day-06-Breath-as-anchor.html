Read · Then Work

For five days you have been observing. You have a trigger catalogue, a performance gap analysis, a map of the identity beliefs that shape how you behave under pressure. Phase 2 does not revisit any of it. It builds on it.

The first tool is not cognitive. It is not a reframe, a question bank, or a conversation framework. Those come. But there is a sequencing problem that most training ignores, and it determines whether everything that follows works or fails.

The prefrontal cortex handles reasoning, language, decision-making, and access to learned strategies. It is also the first region to lose capacity when the stress response activates. When a buyer says "we've already got something for that" and something tightens in your chest — that is not a moment for your best thinking. That is your nervous system assessing threat and routing resources away from the cognitive systems you need most.

The practical consequence: you cannot execute a cognitive strategy from an unregulated physiological state. The script, the framework, the objection response you rehearsed — all of it becomes less accessible the moment your threat response fires. Not because you forgot it. Because the nervous system is not interested in your sales technique right now. It is interested in survival.

Physiological regulation comes first because it is the only sequence that works. Not as a wellness practice. As access to performance.

The tool is cyclic sighing. Here is the exact technique.

Two sharp inhales through the nose — the first to begin filling the lungs, the second to fully inflate them including the lower lobes. Then one slow, extended exhale through the mouth. The double inhale maximises the volume of air before the exhale. The exhale is the active element. It activates the vagus nerve, which signals the parasympathetic nervous system to begin down-regulating the stress response — slowing heart rate, lowering blood pressure, reducing cortisol. The effect begins within two cycles. Five cycles takes under 90 seconds.

Do this before every call today.

There is a second use of this technique that changes how useful it is. It can be deployed silently during a live conversation. One cycle, executed slowly and without visible effort. The inhales through the nose are quiet. The exhale can be slow enough to be imperceptible. A buyer says something that activates you — a direct challenge, a competitor reference, a price objection. One cycle. Return to full capacity. The tool is not only a pre-call protocol. It is available mid-conversation, exactly when you need it.

Today you begin designing your pre-call ritual. It has three components: breath, posture, and intention. Day 07 goes into posture in depth. Intention is addressed in the workbook below. The three components together form a personal entry gate — a 3-minute protocol you use before every call from here forward. Consistency in the ritual builds the nervous system response faster than duration. Three minutes, every time, matters more than ten minutes occasionally.

The Science

In 2023, researchers at Stanford University ran a randomised controlled trial with 108 participants over 28 days. They compared three breathing techniques against mindfulness meditation across primary outcomes: mood, anxiety reduction, and physiological arousal.

Cyclic sighing — two sharp inhales through the nose followed by one long slow exhale through the mouth — produced greater improvement in mood and greater reduction in physiological arousal than every other technique, including mindfulness meditation.

The mechanism: the extended exhale activates the parasympathetic nervous system through the vagus nerve. The vagus nerve is the primary pathway for the parasympathetic branch — it signals the body to slow the heart, lower blood pressure, and reduce cortisol. The effect starts in two cycles. Five cycles before a call takes under 90 seconds. You enter the conversation with full cognitive capacity available, not fighting your own threat response.

Balban et al., Cell Reports Medicine, 2023

You cannot regulate a conversation you have not first regulated yourself into.

Today's Practice Drill
Drill · Pre-Call Ritual — First Deployment

Run your three-component ritual before every call today. Including internal calls. Including calls you expect to be easy. Especially those.

After each call, note one thing: what was your state at the start of the call, and did it hold?

You are not assessing yourself. You are collecting data. What you notice today informs how you refine the ritual over the next six days.

If you have no calls scheduled today, run the ritual three times before each session of work that requires focus. The habit forms the same way. 90 sec pre-call · 3 min post-call debrief
